Protest against Narcotics Bill:
Wakiso Miraa growers and traders threatened to demonstrate if president Yoweri Museveni assented to the Narcotics Drug and Psychotropic Substances Control Bill,2023.The Bill,which bans the cultivation and trading of khat,has been met with resistance. The Association urged that the bill would lead to significant economic losses to miraa farmers and traders.The Association has called for a more naunced approach to regulating the miraa industry.The Association urged the government to reconsider the bill and engage in further consultations with industry stakeholders.
Constitutional Court ruling:
The constitutional court annulled the narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Control Act in May 2023.The Court cited flaws in the parliamentary process, including inadequate consultation with the stakeholders.The ruling was seen as a major victory for the miraa industry,which had challenged the law.The court's decision has thrown the future of the miraa industry into uncertainity. The government has been criticized for its handling of the Bill and the court's ruling.The miraa industry has called for a new, more inclusive approach to regulating the industry.The ruling has set a precedent for future challenges to laws affecting the miraa industry.
Petition to President Museveni:
The association presented a petition to President Museveni amidst tight security, requesting that Khat be removed from the list of prohibited plants in the Act. The petition was signed by thousands of miraa farmers and traders.The association argued that khat is a legitimate crop that supports the livelihoods of many ugandans and its ban would lead to significant economic losses and social unrest.The association called for a swift response to the petition.The fate of the miraa industry hangs in the balance as the government considers the petition.
Call for Regulation:
Medford Lubega Sseggona, the Busiro East member of the parliament, advised the law should only regulate khat consumption,cultivation and value addition.Sseggona urged that a total ban on khat would be counter-productive and lead to illegal trade.He claimed that Regulation would ensure that khat is cultivated and traded in a safe and controlled environment.His call for regulation was supported by several other MPS.Sseggona's comments have sparked a renewed debate on the future of the miraa industry.The association has hailed Sseggona's comments as a welcome contribution to the debate.The future of the miraa industry remains uncertain as the government considers its next move.
